# Prompt

Research ${QUESTION} and produce a decision-grade brief.

1. Scope it: restate the question, the decision it informs, and the constraints that matter.
2. Gather from multiple independent sources; prefer primary/authoritative over aggregators. Note the date of each — flag anything that may be stale.
3. Cross-verify key claims across sources; call out where they disagree and which you trust and why.
4. Synthesize into: the answer up front, then the reasoning, then trade-offs/risks, then what you're still uncertain about.
5. Cite sources inline for every load-bearing claim. Distinguish fact from inference from opinion.

Do not pad. If the evidence is thin or conflicting, say so rather than manufacturing confidence.

# When to use

A consequential question (tech choice, approach, market/standard) where you want sourced, verified findings instead of a confident monologue.
